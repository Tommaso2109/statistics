Brownian Motion (Continuous time & state) — interactive

This interactive demonstrates a standard Brownian motion (Wiener process) on [0,T]. We simulate by dividing [0,T] into n steps of size Δt = T/n, and generate independent normal increments ΔW ~ N(0, Δt) using the Box–Muller transform (uniform → normal). Paths are constructed by cumulative summation of increments; multiple sample paths can be plotted and a histogram of increments is shown with the theoretical Normal overlay.

Notes: Standard Brownian motion W(t) has independent increments and W(t)-W(s) ~ N(0, t-s). We simulate increments with variance σ²Δt by generating normals via Box–Muller from Uniform(0,1) pairs.
